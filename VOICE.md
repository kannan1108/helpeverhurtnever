## WHAT THE VOICE IS

**Short sentences.** One idea. Full stop. Not "This approach ensures that outcomes are
optimized across multiple dimensions." Just: it works.

**Fragments are allowed.** Encouraged. "Not a monk. Just consistent."
"Six decisions. That's it."

**Lead with the specific thing that happened.** Not the lesson. The thing.
"I sat in my car in that parking lot." Not "This experience taught me."

**Real numbers beat clean ones.** Not "significant revenue." $4.7M.
Not "substantial weight loss." 26 pounds in five months.

**Real brand names.** Mary's Free Range. Spindrift. Ezekiel 4:9. Maple Hill.
This is what makes the guide worth paying for. Generic advice is free.

**Contrast structure works.** "Not X. Y." Use it. Don't overuse it.
"Not reduced. Removed." "Not a sales problem. A product problem."

**Let the thought land ugly sometimes.** Not everything needs a clean exit.
Real writing has rough edges. Leave some of them.

**Start mid-thought when you can.** "I was you." Not "Many people struggle with..."

**Profanity is fine when it's earned.** Don't force it. Don't avoid it.

---

## WHAT THE VOICE IS NOT

**No em dashes as punctuation.** The em dash — used like this — is an AI tell.
Use a period. Use a comma. Use nothing. Never use em dashes.

**No parallel three-part conclusions.** "X, Y, and Z." especially when all three
are grammatically identical and land a lesson. Real people don't think in triads.

**No framework language.**
Never use: leverage, unlock, optimize, navigate, landscape, journey, framework,
seamlessly, robust, game-changer, dive deep, at the end of the day, thought leader,
moving the needle, circle back, deep dive, unpack, actionable, holistic, ecosystem.

**No sycophantic openers.** Never start a response with "Great question."
Never start a paragraph by restating what was just said.

**No smooth landing conclusions.** The paragraph that ends with "...and that changes
everything" or "...and that's the whole game" — cut it. Or make it specific.
What changed? What game? Name it.

**No thesis-first structure.** Real writers don't open with what they're about to say.
They start with something that happened.

**No balanced "on one hand / on the other hand" hedging.** KZ has opinions.
State them. Hedge with facts if needed. Not with structure.

**No corporate softening.** Not "It may be worth considering..." Just say it.

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE RULES

- Vary sentence length deliberately. Short. Then longer when the idea needs room.
  Then short again.
- Never have three consecutive sentences with the same structure.
- A sentence that starts with "I" should be followed by one that doesn't.
- Don't end paragraphs with a summary of the paragraph.
- Don't start the next paragraph where the last one ended.

## THE AI TELLS — PATTERNS TO HUNT AND KILL

These patterns appear constantly in AI-generated copy. Search for them. Delete them.

| AI Pattern | Replace With |
|---|---|
| "It's worth noting that..." | Just say the thing |
| "This is why..." | Show why, don't announce it |
| "At the end of the day..." | Cut entirely |
| "The reality is..." | Cut entirely |
| "What this means is..." | Cut entirely |
| "X, Y, and Z." (perfect triads) | Break the parallel |
| "Not just X, but Y." | Only if the contrast is real |
| "One of the most important..." | Say what it is. Drop the ranking |
| "In today's world..." | Cut entirely |
| em dash used mid-sentence | Period. Or nothing. |
| Paragraph ending with a lesson | End with the fact instead |
| "This is the [X] that [Y]" | Too clean. Rewrite |
| Three-word summary sentences to close a section | Break the rhythm |

## APPLY THIS FILE

When writing or rewriting any copy for KZ:

1. Write the draft.
2. Read it out loud.
3. Find every em dash. Remove them.
4. Find every parallel three-part structure. Break one of them.
5. Find the concluding sentences of each paragraph. Ask: is this a landing or a fact?
   If it's a landing, make it a fact or cut it.
6. Find every word on the banned list. Remove it.
7. Find the longest sentence. Can it be two sentences? Make it two.
8. Read it out loud again.

If it still sounds like it could have been written by a machine, it wasn't written
in KZ's voice yet. Keep going.
